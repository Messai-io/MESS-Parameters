# Microbial Species

## Definition

Microbial species identifies the specific microorganisms used as biocatalysts in microbial electrochemical systems (MES). The choice of microbial species directly determines the electron transfer mechanism, substrate utilization pathway, and overall bioelectrocatalytic performance. Exoelectrogenic species capable of extracellular electron transfer (EET) are critical for anode-based systems, while specific acetogens or methanogens may be targeted in cathode-driven processes.

## Typical Values

- **Primary Exoelectrogens**: *Geobacter sulfurreducens*, *Shewanella oneidensis*
- **Mixed Culture Sources**: Activated sludge, anaerobic digester effluent, sediment inocula
- **Engineered Strains**: *E. coli* (with Mtr pathway), *Pseudomonas aeruginosa*

**Common Species by Application**:

- **MFC Anodes**: *Geobacter sulfurreducens* PCA, *Shewanella oneidensis* MR-1
- **MEC Cathodes**: *Methanobacterium palustre*, *Sporomusa ovata*
- **Biosensors**: *Shewanella loihica* PV-4, *Geobacter* spp.
- **Bioremediation**: *Desulfovibrio desulfuricans*, *Geobacter metallireducens*

## Measurement Methods

### Identification Techniques

1. **16S rRNA Gene Sequencing**:
   - Extract DNA from biofilm samples
   - Amplify 16S rRNA gene via PCR
   - Sequence and compare against databases (NCBI, SILVA)

2. **Metagenomics**:
   - Shotgun sequencing of total community DNA
   - Taxonomic profiling and functional annotation
   - Reveals full community composition and metabolic potential

3. **Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH)**:
   - Use fluorescent probes targeting species-specific rRNA
   - Visualize spatial distribution within biofilm

## Affecting Factors

### Primary Factors

1. **Substrate Type**: Carbon source determines which species thrive; acetate favors *Geobacter*, lactate favors *Shewanella*
2. **pH**: Most exoelectrogens prefer near-neutral pH (6.5-7.5)
3. **Temperature**: Mesophilic (25-37°C) conditions favor common exoelectrogens
4. **Oxygen Level**: Strict anaerobes (*Geobacter*) require anoxic conditions; facultative anaerobes (*Shewanella*) tolerate low O₂

## Performance Impact

Species selection is one of the most critical determinants of MES performance. Pure cultures of *Geobacter sulfurreducens* can achieve current densities up to 7 A/m², while mixed communities from wastewater typically produce 0.5-3 A/m². The electron transfer mechanism (direct contact via nanowires vs. mediated shuttle) varies by species and profoundly affects coulombic efficiency and internal resistance.
