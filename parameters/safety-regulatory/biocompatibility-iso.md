# Biocompatibility ISO

## Definition

Biocompatibility ISO refers to compliance with the International Organization
for Standardization (ISO) 10993 series standards for evaluating the
biocompatibility of medical devices and materials used in microbial
electrochemical systems (MES) intended for biomedical applications. This
parameter ensures that MES components that may contact human tissue, blood, or
other biological systems meet stringent safety requirements. It is particularly
critical for implantable MES devices, wearable biosensors, and biomedical power
sources.

**ISO 10993 Compliance Levels:**

- **Level 0**: No testing/Not applicable
- **Level 1**: Limited contact (<24 hours)
- **Level 2**: Prolonged contact (24 hours to 30 days)
- **Level 3**: Permanent contact (>30 days)
- **Level 4**: Implant grade (permanent implantation)

## Typical Requirements

Based on MES biomedical applications:

- **Valid Range**: Level 0-4
- **Research Prototypes**: Level 0-1
- **Wearable Devices**: Level 1-2
- **Temporary Implants**: Level 2-3
- **Permanent Implants**: Level 4
- **Blood-Contacting**: Level 3-4 minimum

### Application Categories:

- **External Skin Contact**: ISO 10993-10 (irritation/sensitization)
- **Mucosal Contact**: ISO 10993-10, 10993-11 (systemic toxicity)
- **Blood Path Contact**: ISO 10993-4 (hemocompatibility)
- **Implantable Devices**: Full ISO 10993 series
- **Biodegradable Systems**: ISO 10993-9 (degradation products)

## ISO 10993 Test Requirements

### Biological Evaluation Tests

1. **ISO 10993-5: Cytotoxicity**:

   - In vitro cell culture tests
   - Extract/direct contact methods
   - Cell viability >70% required
   - L929 mouse fibroblast standard

2. **ISO 10993-10: Irritation/Sensitization**:

   - Skin irritation testing
   - Intracutaneous reactivity
   - Guinea pig maximization test
   - Human patch testing option

3. **ISO 10993-11: Systemic Toxicity**:
   - Acute systemic toxicity
   - Subchronic toxicity (≤28 days)
   - Chronic toxicity (>28 days)
   - Dose-response evaluation

### Specialized Tests

1. **ISO 10993-3: Genotoxicity**:

   - Ames mutagenicity test
   - Chromosomal aberration
   - Mouse lymphoma assay
   - Required for permanent contact

2. **ISO 10993-4: Hemocompatibility**:

   - Hemolysis testing (<5% threshold)
   - Thrombogenicity assessment
   - Complement activation
   - Platelet/leukocyte activation

3. **ISO 10993-6: Local Effects**:
   - Implantation testing
   - Histopathological evaluation
   - Inflammation scoring
   - Tissue integration assessment

## Material Considerations

### Approved Materials for MES

1. **Electrode Materials**:

   - Medical grade titanium
   - Platinum-iridium alloys
   - Medical grade stainless steel (316L)
   - Carbon materials (with coating)

2. **Membrane Materials**:

   - Medical grade silicone
   - Polyurethane (MDX4-4210)
   - PTFE/ePTFE
   - Nafion (with restrictions)

3. **Encapsulation**:
   - Medical grade epoxy
   - Parylene coating
   - Silicone elastomers
   - Hermetic sealing (titanium)

### Restricted Materials

- **Heavy Metals**: Lead, mercury, cadmium
- **Toxic Catalysts**: Unencapsulated transition metals
- **Degradable Polymers**: Unless ISO 10993-9 compliant
- **Nanomaterials**: Additional ISO/TR 10993-22 requirements

## Testing Protocols

### Sample Preparation

1. **Extraction Conditions**:

   - Polar solvent: Saline or water
   - Non-polar solvent: Vegetable oil
   - Temperature: 37°C, 50°C, 70°C, or 121°C
   - Time: 24-72 hours typical
   - Surface area/volume ratio: 6 cm²/mL

2. **Sterilization Impact**:
   - Test post-sterilization
   - Multiple cycles if reusable
   - Ethylene oxide residuals (ISO 10993-7)
   - Gamma irradiation effects

### Documentation Requirements

- **Device Master File**: Complete test records
- **Biological Evaluation Plan**: Risk-based approach
- **Test Reports**: Accredited laboratory
- **Certificate of Compliance**: ISO 10993 declaration

## Risk Assessment (ISO 10993-1)

### Evaluation Framework

1. **Material Characterization**:

   - Chemical composition
   - Manufacturing additives
   - Processing residuals
   - Degradation products

2. **Body Contact Category**:

   - Surface device
   - External communicating
   - Implant device

3. **Contact Duration**:
   - Limited (≤24 hours)
   - Prolonged (>24 hours to 30 days)
   - Permanent (>30 days)

### Risk Mitigation

- **Material Selection**: Use predicate materials
- **Surface Treatment**: Biocompatible coatings
- **Encapsulation**: Hermetic sealing
- **Quality Control**: ISO 13485 compliance

### Design Considerations

- **Minimize Contact Area**: Reduce exposure

- **Consider Alternatives**: In vitro over in vivo
- **Early Testing**: Screen materials early
