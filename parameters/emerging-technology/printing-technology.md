# Printing Technology

Printing technology specifies the additive manufacturing method used to fabricate components of a microbial electrochemical system. Different 3D printing technologies offer distinct capabilities in terms of material compatibility, resolution, speed, mechanical properties, and electrical conductivity. The choice determines which MES components can be printed (electrodes, reactor bodies, flow channels, gaskets) and the achievable performance.

## Typical Values

- **text**: | Technology | Materials | Resolution | MES Application |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| FDM | Thermoplastics, conductive composites | 100 - 400 um | Reactor bodies, frames |
| SLA/DLP | Photopolymers | 25 - 100 um | Detailed reactor components |
| SLS/SLM | Metals, nylon | 20 - 200 um | Metal current collectors |
| DIW | Pastes, hydrogels, carbon inks | 100 - 500 um | Electrode scaffolds |
| Inkjet | Nanoparticle inks | 10 - 50 um | Thin-film electrodes |
| Screen printing | Pastes, thick-film inks | 10 - 100 um | Electrode coatings |

## Measurement Methods

- **1. **Dimensional metrology**: Measure printed part dimensions with calipers or optical scanning.**: 2. **Surface profilometry**: Measure surface roughness (Ra) of printed parts. 3. **Mechanical testing**: Tensile and flexural tests per ASTM standards. 4. **Electrical characterization**: Four-point probe for conductivity of printed electrodes.

## Affecting Factors

- **text**: - **Material requirements**: Desired properties narrow the technology choice.
- **Resolution needs**: Features <100 um require SLA, DLP, inkjet, or aerosol jet.
- **Production volume**: Inkjet and screen printing scale better for production.
- **Cost**: FDM is lowest cost; SLM is highest but produces functional metals.
- **Multi-material capability**: Inkjet and DIW can print multiple materials per build.
