# Nanoparticle Size

Nanoparticle size specifies the characteristic dimension of nanomaterials used to enhance electrode, membrane, or catalyst performance in microbial electrochemical systems. Size governs the surface-area-to-volume ratio, quantum confinement effects, catalytic activity per mass, dispersibility, biofilm interaction, and potential toxicity to electroactive microorganisms. The most dramatic property changes occur below 10 nm.

| MWCNT | 5 - 50 nm diameter | Electrode scaffold |
| Graphene | 1 - 10 nm thickness | Surface area enhancement |
| MnO2 nanostructures | 20 - 200 nm | ORR catalyst |

## Measurement Methods

- **1. **TEM image analysis**: Measure diameter of >100 particles for statistical distribution.**: 2. **DLS**: Hydrodynamic diameter in solution; fast, non-destructive. 3. **XRD Scherrer analysis**: Average crystallite size from peak broadening. 4. **BET surface area**: Calculate equivalent diameter from d = 6/(rho x S_BET). 5. **NTA**: Track individual particle Brownian motion for size distribution.

## Affecting Factors

- **text**: - **Synthesis conditions**: Temperature, pH, and precursor concentration control particle size.
- **Stabilizer**: Capping agents arrest growth at specific sizes.
- **Size-dependent catalysis**: Activity peaks at optimal sizes due to surface atom coordination.
- **Biocompatibility**: Particles <10 nm may penetrate cell membranes and exhibit toxicity.
- **Agglomeration**: Without stabilization, particles aggregate to form larger effective sizes.
