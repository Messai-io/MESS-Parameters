# Nanoparticle Shape

Nanoparticle shape describes the morphology of nanoscale materials used to modify electrodes, membranes, or catalysts in microbial electrochemical systems. Shape profoundly influences surface area, catalytic activity, electron transfer properties, dispersion behavior, and interaction with biofilms. Shape-controlled synthesis enables tuning of exposed crystal facets and aspect ratios to optimize specific electrochemical functions.

| Dendritic | Au, Pt, Pd | Very high surface area |
| Core-shell | Fe@C, Pt@Pd | Cost reduction, synergistic catalysis |

## Measurement Methods

- **1. **TEM imaging**: Direct visualization at nm resolution for shape determination.**: 2. **SEM**: Surface morphology for larger or surface-deposited nanostructures. 3. **DLS**: Hydrodynamic diameter indicates shape effects on diffusion. 4. **SAXS**: Ensemble-averaged shape information from scattering patterns. 5. **AFM**: Height and lateral dimensions for individual particles.

## Affecting Factors

- **text**: - **Synthesis method**: Different routes produce different shapes.
- **Capping agents**: Preferential facet adsorption directs anisotropic growth.
- **Temperature/pH**: Control nucleation and growth kinetics.
- **Precursor concentration**: Affects growth isotropy.
- **Seed-mediated growth**: Pre-formed seeds template further growth.
