# Power Factor

Power factor in MES context refers to the ratio of useful power delivered to the external load to the total electrochemical power generated within the cells. This parameter encompasses all losses between power generation (at the electrode-electrolyte interfaces) and power utilization (at the external load), including ohmic losses within the cell, interconnection losses, and power electronics conversion losses. For DC systems (which all MES are at the cell level), power factor is more accurately described as system efficiency rather than the traditional AC power factor (cos phi), although the latter becomes relevant when MES output is converted to AC via inverters.

The overall power factor of an MES can be decomposed into: electrochemical efficiency (voltage efficiency * coulombic efficiency), internal ohmic efficiency (fraction of EMF not lost to internal resistance), interconnection efficiency (fraction not lost in wiring and contacts), and power electronics efficiency (DC-DC converter and/or inverter losses). Typical MFC electrochemical voltage efficiencies are 20--50% (cell voltage of 0.3--0.6 V relative to the thermodynamic maximum of approximately 1.1 V), internal ohmic efficiencies are 60--90%, interconnection efficiencies are 85--99%, and power electronics efficiencies are 80--95%.

## Typical Values

- **Valid Range**: 0.01 -- 0.99
- **Voltage efficiency (V_cell / E_emf)**: 0.2 -- 0.55
- **Internal ohmic efficiency**: 0.6 -- 0.9
- **Interconnection efficiency**: 0.85 -- 0.99
- **DC-DC converter efficiency**: 0.80 -- 0.95
- **Overall system power factor**: 0.05 -- 0.40
- **Best-case lab MFC**: 0.3 -- 0.4
- **Practical pilot MFC**: 0.1 -- 0.25

## Measurement Methods

- **Component-Level Power Analysis**: Each efficiency component is measured independently. Voltage efficiency = V_cell / E_emf (E_emf from OCV corrected for concentration effects). Ohmic efficiency from EIS (R_ohmic / R_total). Interconnection efficiency from voltage drop measurements. Converter efficiency from input/output power measurements.
- **System-Level Power Measurement**: Gross electrochemical power = sum of (E_emf * I) for all cells. Net delivered power = V_load * I_load. Power factor = P_load / P_electrochemical. This provides the integrated system metric without requiring component-level instrumentation.

## Affecting Factors

### Primary

- **Internal Resistance**: Lower internal resistance (achieved through thin reactors, conductive electrodes, high-conductivity electrolyte) improves voltage efficiency and overall power factor.
- **Operating Current**: Power factor varies with operating point. Maximum power factor occurs near open circuit (low current, high voltage efficiency) but maximum power occurs at intermediate current.
- **Power Electronics Selection**: High-efficiency boost converters (> 90%) are essential for preserving the limited power generated by MES.
- **Interconnection Quality**: Clean, low-resistance connections maintain interconnection efficiency > 95%.
