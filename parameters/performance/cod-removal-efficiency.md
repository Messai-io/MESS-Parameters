# COD Removal Efficiency

## Definition

COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand) removal efficiency represents the percentage of
organic matter removed from wastewater through the bioelectrochemical process in
MFC/MEC/MDC systems. It quantifies the system's ability to treat wastewater
while simultaneously generating electricity or valuable products.

**Formula:** COD Removal Efficiency (%) = [(CODin - CODout) / CODin] × 100

Where:

- CODin = Influent COD concentration (mg/L)
- CODout = Effluent COD concentration (mg/L)

## Typical Values

**Range:** 60-95% **Typical:** 80-85% **Outlier Threshold:** <50% or >98%

### Performance Categories:

- **Excellent:** 90-95% (Advanced systems with optimized biofilms)
- **Good:** 80-90% (Well-maintained commercial systems)
- **Moderate:** 70-80% (Standard laboratory systems)
- **Poor:** <70% (Unoptimized or failing systems)

## Measurement Methods

### Standard Methods

1. **Closed Reflux Method (Standard Method 5220 C)**

   - Most accurate for MFC effluents
   - Requires 2-3 hours digestion
   - Detection limit: 5-10 mg/L

2. **Open Reflux Method (Standard Method 5220 B)**

   - Suitable for high COD samples
   - More economical for routine monitoring
   - Detection limit: 50 mg/L

3. **Colorimetric Method**
   - Rapid analysis (15-120 minutes)
   - Commercial test kits available
   - Range: 0-1500 mg/L typically

### Online Monitoring

1. **UV-Vis Spectroscopy**

   - Real-time monitoring capability
   - Correlation with COD established
   - Wavelength: 254 nm typically

2. **Electrochemical Sensors**
   - Continuous measurement
   - Based on oxidation current
   - Response time: <5 minutes

## Affecting Factors

### Primary Factors

- **Hydraulic Retention Time (HRT):** Longer HRT generally increases removal
  efficiency
- **Organic Loading Rate:** Higher loading can decrease efficiency
- **Temperature:** Optimal range 25-35°C for mesophilic bacteria
- **pH:** Optimal range 6.5-8.0 for most systems
- **Dissolved Oxygen:** Should be minimal in anode chamber (<0.5 mg/L)

### Secondary Factors

- **Substrate Complexity:** Simple substrates (acetate) show higher removal than
  complex organics
- **Biofilm Maturity:** Mature biofilms (>30 days) show better removal
- **Internal Resistance:** Lower resistance improves electron recovery and COD
  removal
- **Mixing/Flow Pattern:** Proper mixing prevents dead zones
- **Toxic Compounds:** Heavy metals or antibiotics can inhibit removal

## Performance Impact

### System Performance Metrics

- **Power Density:** Higher COD removal often correlates with higher power
  output
- **Coulombic Efficiency:** COD consumed for electricity vs. other pathways
- **Methane Production:** In MECs, affects overall COD removal
- **Biomass Yield:** Lower yield means more COD converted to electricity

### Operational Stability

- Consistent COD removal indicates stable microbial community
- Sudden drops may indicate system failure or toxic shock
- Gradual decline suggests biofilm aging or substrate limitations

## Compatible Systems

### Operating Conditions

- **Temperature Range:** 15-40°C (mesophilic), 50-60°C (thermophilic)
- **pH Range:** 5.5-9.0 (optimal 6.8-7.2)
- **Salinity:** Up to 35 g/L NaCl for marine applications
- **Flow Rate:** 0.1-10 mL/min for lab scale

### Applications

- **Municipal Wastewater:** 70-85% removal typical
- **Industrial Wastewater:** 60-90% depending on composition
- **Agricultural Wastewater:** 75-95% for animal waste
- **Food Processing:** 80-95% for brewery/dairy waste

## Optimization Strategies

1. **Design Optimization**

   - Increase electrode surface area
   - Reduce electrode spacing
   - Optimize reactor configuration

2. **Operational Optimization**

   - Control external resistance
   - Maintain optimal pH
   - Regular biofilm maintenance

3. **Biological Enhancement**
   - Bioaugmentation with efficient strains
   - Co-substrate addition
   - Nutrient supplementation

## Validation Rules

### Critical Limits

- Minimum: 0% (system failure)
- Maximum: 100% (theoretical limit)
- Typical operational range: 60-95%

### Quality Checks

- Mass balance verification
- Comparison with BOD removal
- Electron balance calculation
- Carbon balance assessment
