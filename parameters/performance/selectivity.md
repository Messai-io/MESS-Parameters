# Selectivity

## Definition

Selectivity in microbial electrochemical systems refers to the preferential
production of a specific product or the selective removal of target compounds
from a mixture. In MECs, it typically describes the ratio of desired product
(e.g., H₂, CH₄, or specific chemicals) to total products formed. In MFCs and
MDCs, it can refer to selective substrate utilization or ion transport.

**Formula:** Selectivity (%) = (Desired Product / Total Products) × 100

For electrochemical reactions: Selectivity = (Current for desired reaction /
Total current) × 100

## Typical Values

**Range:** 50-99% **Typical:** 70-90% **Outlier Threshold:** <40% or >99.5%

### Selectivity by Product Type:

- **H₂ in MEC:** 85-95% (vs. methane)
- **CH₄ in MEC:** 80-90% (vs. hydrogen)
- **Acetate Production:** 70-85% (from CO₂)
- **Ion Selectivity (MDC):** 60-80% (specific ions)

## Measurement Methods

### Product Analysis Methods

1. **Gas Chromatography**

   - H₂, CH₄, CO₂ quantification
   - High precision (±1%)
   - Automated sampling possible
   - Industry standard method

2. **Mass Spectrometry**

   - Real-time gas analysis
   - Isotope labeling studies
   - Trace product detection
   - Reaction pathway elucidation

3. **Liquid Chromatography**
   - Organic acid products
   - Sugar consumption profiles
   - Intermediate detection
   - Concentration profiles

### Electrochemical Methods

1. **Faradaic Efficiency**

   - Electron accountability
   - Product-specific calculations
   - Current integration
   - Coulombic selectivity

2. **Differential Electrochemical Mass Spectrometry (DEMS)**
   - Online product detection
   - Potential-dependent selectivity
   - Reaction onset identification
   - Mechanistic insights

## Affecting Factors

### Primary Factors

- **Applied Potential (MEC):** -0.6 V for H₂, -0.8 V for CH₄
- **pH:** Affects product distribution and microbial activity
- **Substrate Type:** Complex substrates reduce selectivity
- **Microbial Community:** Pure vs. mixed cultures
- **Temperature:** Thermodynamic and kinetic effects

### Secondary Factors

- **Retention Time:** Longer HRT may reduce selectivity
- **Partial Pressure:** Product accumulation effects
- **Inhibitors:** Selective microbial inhibition
- **Membrane Type:** Ion selectivity in MDCs
- **Buffer Capacity:** pH stability affects selectivity

## Performance Impact

### Economic Implications

- **Product Purity:** Higher selectivity reduces separation costs
- **Energy Efficiency:** Selective processes minimize energy waste
- **Process Control:** Easier operation with high selectivity
- **Market Value:** Pure products command higher prices

### System Design Considerations

- Reactor configuration affects selectivity
- Electrode materials influence product distribution
- Operating conditions optimize for target product
- Separation requirements depend on selectivity

## Compatible Systems

### MEC Applications

- **H₂ Production:**

  - Potential: -0.6 to -0.8 V
  - pH: 5.5-7.0
  - Temperature: 30-35°C
  - Selectivity: >90%

- **CH₄ Production:**
  - Potential: -0.8 to -1.0 V
  - pH: 6.8-7.5
  - Temperature: 35-37°C
  - Selectivity: >85%

### MDC Applications

- **Monovalent Ion Selectivity:**
  - Na⁺ over Ca²⁺: 70-80%
  - Cl⁻ over SO₄²⁻: 65-75%
- **Specific Ion Removal:**
  - NO₃⁻ selectivity: 60-70%
  - PO₄³⁻ selectivity: 55-65%

## Optimization Strategies

1. **Microbial Selection**

   - Pure culture inoculation
   - Selective enrichment
   - Bioaugmentation
   - Synthetic consortia

2. **Operational Control**

   - Potential optimization
   - pH regulation
   - Temperature control
   - Substrate feeding strategy

3. **System Engineering**
   - Membrane selection
   - Electrode modification
   - Reactor design
   - Product removal rate

## Validation Rules

### Measurement Requirements

- Carbon balance: >95%
- Electron balance: >90%
- Reproducibility: ±5%
- Steady-state operation

### Quality Indicators

- Stable selectivity over time
- Consistent with thermodynamics
- Mass balance closure
- No unexplained products
