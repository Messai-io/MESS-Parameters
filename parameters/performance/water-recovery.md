# Water Recovery

## Definition

Water recovery represents the percentage of water that can be reclaimed and
reused from the effluent stream of a microbial electrochemical system (MES).
This parameter is particularly important for integrated wastewater treatment and
water reclamation applications, where MES technology serves dual purposes of
energy generation and water resource recovery. It quantifies the system's
effectiveness in producing reusable water while simultaneously treating waste
and generating electricity.

**Formula:** WR = (V_recovered / V_influent) × 100%

Where:

- WR = Water recovery percentage (%)
- V_recovered = Volume of water meeting reuse standards (L)
- V_influent = Total influent volume processed (L)

## Typical Values

Based on MES water treatment applications:

- **Valid Range**: 0 - 99%
- **Typical Range**: 40 - 85%
- **Low Recovery**: 20 - 40% (high strength wastewater)
- **Moderate Recovery**: 40 - 70% (municipal wastewater)
- **High Recovery**: 70 - 85% (dilute streams)
- **Maximum Recovery**: 85 - 95% (optimized systems)

### Application Categories:

- **Industrial Wastewater**: 20 - 50%
- **Municipal Wastewater**: 50 - 75%
- **Greywater Treatment**: 70 - 85%
- **Blackwater Treatment**: 30 - 60%
- **Agricultural Runoff**: 60 - 80%

## Measurement Methods

### Volume Measurement

1. **Direct Flow Measurement**:

   - Influent and effluent flow meters
   - Continuous monitoring
   - Most accurate method
   - Real-time data collection

2. **Batch Volume Tracking**:

   - Tank level measurements
   - Batch processing volumes
   - Periodic assessment
   - Simple implementation

3. **Mass Balance Approach**:
   - Account for all water streams
   - Include evaporation losses
   - Consider sludge moisture
   - Comprehensive analysis

### Quality Assessment

- **Turbidity**: <5 NTU for reuse
- **BOD/COD**: Meet discharge standards
- **Pathogen Removal**: >99.9% typical requirement
- **TDS**: Application-specific limits

## Affecting Factors

### Primary Factors

1. **Treatment Efficiency**:

   - COD removal rate
   - Suspended solids removal
   - Pathogen inactivation
   - Nutrient removal

2. **Membrane Configuration**:

   - Ultrafiltration integration
   - Forward osmosis coupling
   - Membrane fouling rate
   - Rejection characteristics

3. **Operating Conditions**:
   - Hydraulic retention time
   - Organic loading rate
   - Temperature effects
   - pH optimization

### System Losses

1. **Evaporation**: 1-5% typical
2. **Sludge Water Content**: 5-15% typical
3. **Backwash Requirements**: 2-5% typical
4. **Sampling/Testing**: <1% typical

## Performance Relationships

### Water Quality vs. Recovery

- **Higher Recovery**: Lower effluent quality
- **Lower Recovery**: Higher effluent quality
- **Optimal Balance**: 60-75% for most applications
- **Energy Trade-off**: Higher recovery requires more energy

### Integration Benefits

1. **MES + Membrane Filtration**:

   - Recovery: 70-90%
   - Quality: Potable standards possible
   - Energy: Partially self-powered

2. **MES + Constructed Wetlands**:
   - Recovery: 60-80%
   - Quality: Irrigation standards
   - Cost: Lower capital investment

## Related Parameters

### Direct Relationships

- **HRT**: Longer retention improves quality
- **COD Removal**: Higher removal enables recovery
- **Power Density**: Energy for pumping/treatment
- **Membrane Flux**: Determines recovery rate

### System Indicators

- **Specific Energy**: kWh/m³ water recovered
- **Recovery Cost**: $/m³ water produced
- **Quality Index**: Compliance with standards

## Optimization Strategies

### Recovery Enhancement

1. **Pre-treatment**:

   - Screening and settling
   - pH adjustment
   - Equalization

2. **Post-treatment**:

   - Membrane filtration
   - Disinfection
   - Nutrient polishing

3. **Process Control**:
   - Automated monitoring
   - Adaptive operation
   - Fouling management

### Energy Balance

- **Energy Generation**: 0.1-0.3 kWh/m³
- **Treatment Energy**: 0.2-0.5 kWh/m³
- **Net Energy**: Often negative but improving
- **Future Target**: Energy-neutral operation

## Application Guidelines

### Water Reuse Standards

1. **Agricultural Irrigation**:

   - BOD < 30 mg/L
   - TSS < 30 mg/L
   - Fecal coliform < 200 CFU/100mL

2. **Industrial Reuse**:

   - Application-specific
   - Often requires RO polishing
   - TDS < 500 mg/L typical

3. **Potable Reuse**:
   - Multiple barrier approach
   - Advanced treatment required
   - Regulatory compliance critical

## Economic Considerations

### Cost Factors

- **Capital Cost**: $500-2000/m³/day capacity
- **Operating Cost**: $0.20-0.50/m³ treated
- **Water Value**: $0.50-5.00/m³ recovered
- **Payback Period**: 5-15 years typical

### Value Streams

1. **Water Sales**: Primary revenue
2. **Energy Recovery**: Offset treatment costs
3. **Nutrient Recovery**: Additional revenue
4. **Carbon Credits**: Emerging market

## Validation Rules

1. **Mass Balance**: Input = Output + Losses
2. **Quality Compliance**: Meet reuse standards
3. **Recovery Limit**: Cannot exceed 100%
4. **Economic Viability**: Positive NPV target
