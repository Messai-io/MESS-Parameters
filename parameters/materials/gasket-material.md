# Gasket Material

## Definition

Gasket material refers to the sealing component used to prevent leakage of electrolyte, gases, and other fluids between chambers and at joints in microbial electrochemical systems (MES). The gasket must provide a reliable seal under operating pressures while being chemically inert to the electrolyte, biocompatible with microbial cultures, and dimensionally stable over the system's operational lifetime. Proper gasket selection prevents cross-contamination between anode and cathode chambers and maintains anaerobic conditions where required.

## Typical Values

- **Silicone Rubber**: Shore A 40-70, temperature range -60 to 200°C
- **EPDM Rubber**: Shore A 50-70, excellent chemical resistance
- **Viton (FKM)**: Shore A 60-90, superior chemical resistance
- **PTFE (Teflon)**: Chemically inert, Shore D 55-65
- **Neoprene**: Shore A 40-70, moderate chemical resistance

**Selection by Application**:

- **Laboratory MFC**: Silicone rubber (easy to cut, autoclavable)
- **Wastewater Systems**: EPDM (acid/base resistant, low cost)
- **High-Temperature MES**: Viton or PTFE
- **Hydrogen-Producing MEC**: PTFE or Viton (low gas permeability)

## Measurement Methods

### Evaluation

1. **Compression Set Testing** (ASTM D395):
   - Compress gasket to 25% deflection for 22 hours at operating temperature
   - Measure permanent deformation after recovery

2. **Chemical Compatibility**:
   - Immerse gasket samples in electrolyte for 7-30 days
   - Measure mass change, hardness change, and dimensional stability

## Affecting Factors

### Primary Factors

1. **Electrolyte pH**: Extreme pH values degrade certain elastomers; PTFE is universally resistant
2. **Gas Permeability**: Hydrogen permeation through gaskets can reduce MEC efficiency
3. **Bolt Torque**: Insufficient compression causes leaks; excessive compression damages gaskets
4. **Biofilm Growth**: Some materials support biofilm colonization on gasket surfaces, potentially causing clogging

## Performance Impact

Gasket failures are a leading cause of MES operational issues. Electrolyte leaks reduce coulombic efficiency by 5-20% through direct substrate loss. Oxygen intrusion through failed seals in anaerobic systems can inhibit obligate anaerobes and reduce power output by 30-50%. Proper gasket selection and compression maintain seal integrity for 6-24 months of continuous operation.
