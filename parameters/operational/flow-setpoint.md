# Flow Setpoint

Flow setpoint is the target volumetric or mass flow rate that the MES flow control system is configured to maintain. This is the commanded value to which the pump speed, valve position, or pressure is adjusted to achieve the desired hydraulic conditions. The flow setpoint directly determines the hydraulic retention time, organic loading rate, and dilution rate of the system, and its accurate maintenance is essential for reproducible and optimized MES performance.

In most laboratory MES, the flow setpoint is achieved by setting the peristaltic pump to a specific RPM derived from a pre-established calibration curve (RPM vs. flow rate for a given tubing type and size). At pilot scale, the setpoint is typically entered into a PID controller that modulates a variable-frequency pump drive or control valve to maintain the measured flow rate at the desired value. The setpoint may be constant (for steady-state operation) or programmed to follow a time-varying profile (for diurnal flow patterns or step-change experiments).

Flow setpoint selection involves trade-offs between treatment efficiency, power output, and operational costs. Lower setpoints (longer HRT) improve COD removal but reduce throughput. Higher setpoints (shorter HRT) increase throughput and volumetric power density but may compromise treatment quality. The optimal setpoint depends on the treatment objectives, substrate characteristics, and biofilm capacity.

## Typical Values

- **Valid Range**: 0.001 -- 10000 mL/min
- **Lab-scale continuous MFC (28 mL reactor)**: 0.02 -- 0.5 mL/min
- **Lab-scale continuous MFC (250 mL reactor)**: 0.1 -- 5 mL/min
- **Bench-scale MFC (5 L)**: 1 -- 50 mL/min
- **Pilot-scale MFC (100 L)**: 50 -- 500 mL/min
- **Recirculation setpoint (lab)**: 1 -- 100 mL/min (5--50x feed rate)
- **Recirculation setpoint (pilot)**: 0.5 -- 50 L/min

## Measurement Methods

- **Pump RPM Calibration**: The flow setpoint is translated to pump RPM using a calibration curve. The curve is generated by measuring actual flow rate at 5+ RPM settings using timed volumetric collection. Calibration accuracy: +/- 2--5%. Must be repeated when tubing is changed (every 500--2000 operating hours).
- **PID Controller Feedback**: A PID controller continuously adjusts the pump speed or control valve position to minimize the difference between the measured flow rate (from an inline flow meter) and the setpoint. The controller parameters (P, I, D gains) are tuned for the specific system. Setpoint accuracy: +/- 0.5--2% for well-tuned systems.
- **Manual Verification**: Regardless of the control method, the actual flow rate should be verified periodically (daily at startup, weekly during steady operation) by timed volumetric collection. If the measured rate differs from the setpoint by > 5%, the pump calibration or controller parameters need adjustment.

## Affecting Factors

### Primary

- **Target HRT**: The flow setpoint is calculated as Q = V / HRT. For a 250 mL reactor at 6 h HRT, the setpoint is 0.69 mL/min. For 24 h HRT, the setpoint is 0.17 mL/min.
- **Tubing Wear**: Peristaltic pump tubing deforms over time, reducing actual flow rate by 5--15% over weeks. The setpoint RPM must be periodically increased to compensate, or the flow must be independently measured and controlled.
- **Backpressure Variations**: For constant-RPM pumps, increasing backpressure (from membrane fouling or outlet restriction) reduces actual flow rate below the setpoint, effectively increasing HRT.
- **Feed Characteristics**: Viscous or high-solids feeds flow more slowly through the same tubing, requiring higher pump RPM to maintain the flow setpoint.
- **Temperature**: Fluid viscosity changes with temperature affect pump delivery rate. Cold feeds require higher RPM for the same setpoint.
