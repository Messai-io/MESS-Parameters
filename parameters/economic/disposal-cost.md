# Disposal Cost

Disposal cost is the expense of managing and disposing of waste streams generated by MES operation, primarily excess biological sludge, spent electrodes, fouled membranes, and chemical wastes. Expressed in USD per m^3 treated or USD per ton of waste, disposal costs are a significant operating expense component. MES technology's inherently low sludge production (60--90% less than activated sludge) provides a major economic advantage because sludge disposal typically represents 30--60% of conventional wastewater treatment operating costs.

Sludge disposal options include landfilling ($30--100/ton in developed countries), incineration ($100--300/ton), composting ($20--80/ton), and land application ($10--40/ton). Spent electrodes (carbon-based) may be landfilled as non-hazardous waste or potentially recycled. Spent membranes (polymer) are typically landfilled. Chemical wastes from cleaning (dilute acid/base solutions) require neutralization before discharge.

## Typical Values

- **Valid Range**: 0 -- 5 USD/m^3 treated
- **MFC sludge disposal**: $0.01 -- 0.10/m^3 treated
- **Activated sludge disposal (reference)**: $0.05 -- 0.50/m^3 treated
- **Sludge disposal (landfill)**: $30 -- 100/wet ton
- **Sludge disposal (incineration)**: $100 -- 300/wet ton
- **Electrode disposal**: $0.001 -- 0.01/m^3 (amortized over replacement interval)
- **Membrane disposal**: $0.001 -- 0.005/m^3 (amortized)
- **MFC cost advantage vs. AS**: 60 -- 90% reduction in sludge disposal cost

## Measurement Methods

- **Waste Stream Quantification**: All waste streams are weighed and characterized (TSS, VSS, moisture content, heavy metals, toxicity). Disposal cost = sum of (waste quantity * disposal unit cost) for all streams over the reporting period.
- **Vendor Contract Analysis**: Disposal costs based on contracted rates with waste haulers, landfill operators, or recycling facilities. Contracts typically specify cost per ton or per container pickup.

## Affecting Factors

### Primary

- **Sludge Production Rate**: MFC sludge yield of 0.05 kg VSS/kg COD vs. AS yield of 0.4 kg VSS/kg COD means 8x less sludge to dispose.
- **Disposal Regulations**: Stricter regulations on sludge contaminants (heavy metals, PFAS) increase disposal costs.
- **Location**: Disposal costs vary 5--10x globally. Urban areas with limited landfill capacity have highest costs.
- **Sludge Dewatering**: Mechanical dewatering to 20--25% solids reduces hauling volume and cost by 5--10x.
