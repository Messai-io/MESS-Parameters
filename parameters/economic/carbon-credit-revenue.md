# Carbon Credit Revenue

Carbon credit revenue is the income generated from selling carbon emission reduction credits (certified emission reductions, CERs, or voluntary carbon offsets) attributable to MES operation. MES can claim carbon credits through three mechanisms: (1) displacement of grid electricity by MFC-generated power, (2) avoidance of methane emissions that would occur in conventional anaerobic treatment (methane has 28x the global warming potential of CO2 over 100 years), and (3) reduced energy consumption compared to aerobic treatment (avoiding CO2 from fossil fuel combustion for aeration).

The carbon credit value varies dramatically by market and certification standard. EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS) credits trade at $50--100/ton CO2-eq, voluntary carbon markets range from $5--50/ton, and some emerging markets are below $5/ton. For MES treating domestic wastewater, the estimated CO2-equivalent reduction is 0.5--2 kg CO2-eq per m^3 treated (compared to activated sludge), yielding potential carbon revenue of $0.005--0.10 per m^3 at current market prices.

While carbon credit revenue alone is unlikely to justify MES investment, it can contribute 5--15% of total revenue in favorable market conditions and helps quantify the environmental benefit of MES technology for sustainability reporting and regulatory compliance.

## Typical Values

- **Valid Range**: 0 -- 10 USD/m^3 treated
- **CO2-eq avoided (MFC vs. activated sludge)**: 0.5 -- 2 kg CO2-eq/m^3
- **EU ETS carbon price**: $50 -- 100/ton CO2-eq (2024)
- **Voluntary market price**: $5 -- 50/ton CO2-eq
- **Revenue per m^3 (EU ETS)**: $0.025 -- 0.20
- **Revenue per m^3 (voluntary)**: $0.003 -- 0.10
- **Fraction of total MES revenue**: 5 -- 15%
- **Annual revenue (1 MLD plant, EU ETS)**: $5000 -- 70000/year

## Measurement Methods

- **Carbon Footprint Calculation**: The net CO2-eq reduction is calculated as the difference between the carbon footprint of conventional treatment and MES treatment for the same functional unit (m^3 treated). Includes: direct emissions (CH4, N2O), indirect emissions (grid electricity), and embodied emissions (materials, chemicals).
- **Certification and Verification**: For tradeable credits, an accredited third-party verifier audits the emission reduction claim per the applicable standard (CDM, Gold Standard, Verified Carbon Standard). Monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) protocols must be established and followed continuously.

## Affecting Factors

### Primary

- **Carbon Price**: Market price volatility directly affects revenue. EU ETS has ranged from $5 to $100/ton over the past decade.
- **Baseline Technology**: Credits are calculated relative to a baseline. Compared to aerobic treatment, MES avoids 0.5--2 kg CO2-eq/m^3. Compared to anaerobic digestion (with biogas recovery), the advantage is smaller.
- **Grid Carbon Intensity**: Displacing coal-heavy grid electricity generates more credits than displacing renewable-heavy grids.
- **Certification Costs**: Third-party verification costs $10,000--50,000 per project, requiring a minimum project scale to be economic.
